What is a portfolio?

A portfolio refers to a collection of investments, assets, or projects held by an individual or organization. The purpose of a portfolio is typically to diversify risk and optimize returns by spreading investments across different asset classes, such as stocks, bonds, real estate, and commodities. Portfolios can also include non-financial assets such as artwork or collectibles.

In the context of employment, a portfolio may refer to a collection of a person's work samples, projects, and achievements that demonstrate their skills and experience in a particular field. A portfolio can be used to showcase an individual's qualifications and achievements when applying for jobs, seeking promotion or new clients, or building their professional reputation.
